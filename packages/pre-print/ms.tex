\title{An Analytic Origin for the Provenance Bias in Radial Migration: A Vertical
Form Factor, Corotation Trapping, and the Spiral $\JR$--$\hZ$ Relation}

\author{Carlos Vera-Ciro}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher}
\email{caverac@gmail.com}

\begin{abstract}
Radial migration in disk galaxies acts preferentially on stars with small vertical
excursions -- the ``provenance bias'' established in $N$-body simulations
\citep{VeraCiro2016imprint,Mikkola2020} but never derived. We supply the analytic
theory. The coupling of a star to a spiral of radial wavenumber $k$ is set by the
angle-average over its vertical orbit of the potential's $e^{-k|z|}$ fall-off above
the plane, a \emph{vertical form factor}
$F(\Jz,k)=\langle e^{-k|z|}\rangle$, which equals unity for a mid-plane orbit and
decreases with vertical action. We show that $F$ is not an \emph{ad hoc} weight but
the exact vertical Fourier coefficient of the spiral in the corotation-resonant
Hamiltonian: the resonant coupling factorizes as
$\Psi=\Phi_0\,J_0(k a_R)\,F(\Jz,k)$, the product of the classical radial reduction
factor and $F$. The corotation pendulum then traps stars in a libration island
whose width scales as $\sqrt{F}$, so vertically cold stars are captured
preferentially -- the provenance bias, derived. We measure the bias by the log
dispersion ratio $\ln\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}$ (negative; more negative is
stronger). Single-resonance trapping floors the bias near ${\sim}-7$ percent, whereas
resonance overlap drives the disk into a diffusive regime with a predicted
${\sim}-20$ to $-27$ percent band; we show with the
Chirikov criterion that the Milky Way bar and spiral corotation resonances sit at
the overlap threshold for physical spiral strengths, with cold stars crossing it
first -- so the diffusive churning channel is itself biased toward vertically cold
stars, matching the strong cold-migrator preference of the simulations.
Because $F$ depends on $k$ and $\hZ$ only through $\alpha\equiv k\hZ$, the same
object links the bias to the empirical spiral $\JR^{\max}$--$\hZ$ relation
\citep{Palicio2024}; the structural slope reproduces the measured
$3.7\times10^{-2}\,\Lsun/\kpc$ and is predicted to flatten with population age. We
validate $F$ against independent orbit integration and provide the open-source
engine that derives every number and figure.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Transient, recurrent spiral arms
\citep{SellwoodCarlberg1984,DOnghia2013,Baba2013,SellwoodCarlberg2014} churn stellar
angular momentum at the corotation resonance, moving stars radially with little
heating \citep{SellwoodBinney2002,Roskar2008}. A robust but only qualitatively
understood feature of this process is its \emph{vertical selectivity}: in $N$-body
disks the stars that migrate are preferentially those on nearly circular orbits
confined to the plane.
\citet{VeraCiro2016imprint} named this the ``provenance bias'' and showed that it --
rather than vertical heating -- sets the vertical structure of migrators: the
strongest migrators are preferentially the kinematically cool stars, those with
vertical velocity dispersions below the local mean (their Fig.~4).
\citet{Mikkola2020} confirmed that the importance of migration at large vertical
action depends on spiral strength, and explicitly flagged an analytic description
as outstanding.

Independently, \citet{Palicio2024} report a linear relation, found across simulated
spiral galaxies and satisfied by the Milky Way, between the maximum radial action
of spiral-arm stars and the disk vertical scale $\hZ$ -- measured there as an
exponential vertical scale-length -- $\JR^{\max}=a\,\hZ+b$ with
$a\approx3.7\times10^{-2}\,\Lsun/\kpc$ (see also \citealt{Jia2024}); the slope is
not derived from first principles.

This paper provides the missing dynamics. \citet{DanielWyse2015,DanielWyse2018}
derived an analytic corotation-capture criterion in which the maximum random energy
for capture scales with the spiral amplitude $|\Phi_s|$, so that low-dispersion
stars migrate preferentially -- but their criterion is strictly in-plane. We extend
the problem to three dimensions and show that the vertical structure enters through
a single object, the vertical form factor $F(\Jz,k)$
(Section~\ref{sec:formfactor}), which we then identify as the exact vertical
Fourier coefficient of the spiral in the corotation-resonant Hamiltonian
(Section~\ref{sec:trapping}). The resulting pendulum traps vertically cold stars
preferentially; single-resonance trapping is too weak to explain the strong
cold-migrator preference of the simulations, and we show
(Section~\ref{sec:overlap}) that the Milky Way sits at the
\citet{Chirikov1979} resonance-overlap threshold, placing the churning in the
diffusive regime. We then recover the provenance-bias magnitude and the
$\JR^{\max}$--$\hZ$ slope and its age dependence (Section~\ref{sec:bias}) and
discuss the synthesis (Section~\ref{sec:discussion}).

\section{The vertical form factor}
\label{sec:formfactor}

A razor-thin spiral perturbation of radial wavenumber $k$ has a potential that
satisfies Laplace's equation above the disk and therefore decays as $e^{-k|z|}$
\citep{BinneyTremaine2008}. A star executing a vertical oscillation
$z(\theta_z;\Jz)$ samples the spiral through the angle-average of that decay,
%
\begin{equation}
  F(\Jz,k) = \big\langle e^{-k|z(\theta_z,\Jz)|}\big\rangle_{\theta_z}
  = \frac{2}{\pi}\int_0^{z_m} \mathrm{d}z\, e^{-kz}\,\frac{\nu_z}{|v_z|},
  \label{eq:formfactor}
\end{equation}
%
with $z_m$ the turning point, $\nu_z$ the vertical frequency, $\Ez$ the conserved
vertical-oscillation energy and $v_z=\sqrt{2[\Ez-\Phi(z)]}$. We evaluate
\eqref{eq:formfactor} on the
self-consistent isothermal sheet \citep{Spitzer1942}, $\Phi(z)=2\ln\cosh z$ in
units $G=\sigz=z_0=1$, for which the only parameter is the dimensionless thickness
$\alpha\equiv kz_0$. By construction $F(0,k)=1$ -- a mid-plane orbit feels the full
in-plane amplitude -- and $F$ decreases monotonically with vertical action
(Figure~\ref{fig:formfactor}). Physically, $F$ is the average amplitude a star
feels -- the time its orbit spends near the plane, weighted by the spiral's
$e^{-k|z|}$ reach. It makes precise the ``spend more time near the mid-plane'' half
of the verbal mechanism; that this orbit-average is itself the coupling strength
entering the corotation resonance is shown in Section~\ref{sec:trapping}.

For the Milky Way a radial wavelength $\lambda_R\sim3\kpc$
($k=2\pi/\lambda_R\sim2\kpc^{-1}$; \citealp{Reid2019}) and a vertical scale
$z_0\sim0.4\kpc$ \citep{BlandHawthornGerhard2016} give $\alpha\sim0.84$ -- of order
unity, so the vertical coupling is appreciably selective rather than negligible; we
quantify the resulting bias in the trapping and overlap analyses below.

  \caption{Vertical form factor $F(\Jz,k)=\langle e^{-k|z|}\rangle$ (Equation~\eqref{eq:formfactor}),
  as a function of vertical action for three spiral
  wavenumbers $\alpha=kz_0$. The value of $\alpha = 0.84$ is a rough approximation for the Milky Way. 
  $F$ falls monotonically with $\Jz$ (vertically hot

  feeling the full amplitude.}
  \label{fig:formfactor}
\end{figure}

\paragraph{Finite-thickness correction.} The $e^{-k|z|}$ kernel is the razor-thin
leading term; the arm overdensity is in fact disk material of finite vertical extent
$h_s$ (comparable to the disk scale $z_0$), and convolving the thin-sheet kernel with
that profile rounds the mid-plane cusp \citep{Toomre1964}. For an exponential arm
profile $g(z)\propto e^{-|z|/h_s}$, with $h_s$ the arm's vertical scale height, the
softened kernel is $K(z)=[\beta e^{-k|z|}-k e^{-\beta|z|}]/(\beta-k)$ with
$\beta=1/h_s$ -- the thin-sheet kernel convolved with $g$ and normalized to $K(0)=1$,
a direct solution of Poisson's equation \citep{BinneyTremaine2008}. Its orbit average
is
%
\begin{equation}
  F_{\rm soft}(\Jz;\alpha,h)=\frac{F(\Jz,\alpha)-\alpha h\,F(\Jz,1/h)}{1-\alpha h},
  \, h\equiv h_s/z_0,
  \label{eq:softened}
\end{equation}
%
a combination of the bare form factor at the two wavenumbers. It recovers $F$ as
$h\to0$ and tends to unity as $h\to\infty$. We use $F_{\rm soft}$ in place of the razor-thin $F$ when evaluating the Milky-Way
provenance bias in Section~\ref{sec:trapping}.

\section{The corotation-resonant Hamiltonian and the trapped weight}
\label{sec:trapping}

We now show that $F$ is not an inserted weight but the vertical structure factor of
corotation capture. Write the background in action-angle variables
$(\JR,\Lz,\Jz;\theta_R,\theta_\phi,\theta_z)$ with frequencies
$(\kappa,\Omega,\nu_z)$. A steady $m$-armed, tightly wound spiral of pattern speed
$\Omega_p$ is
%
\begin{equation}
  \Phi_s = \mathrm{Re}\!\left[\Phi_0(R)\,e^{-k|z|}\,
  e^{\,i(m\phi + \int\! \mathrm{d}R\, k - m\Omega_p t)}\right],
\end{equation}
%
whose Fourier expansion in the background angles,
%
\begin{equation}
  \Phi_s = \sum_{n_R,\,n_z} \Phi_{n_R n_z}(\JR,\Lz,\Jz)\,
  e^{\,i(n_R\theta_R + m\theta_\phi + n_z\theta_z - m\Omega_p t)},
\end{equation}
%
has coefficients that factorize into independent radial and vertical fast-angle
averages; here $n_R$ and $n_z$ are the integer harmonics of the radial and vertical
angles (the azimuthal harmonic is fixed at the arm number $m$). The $(n_R,n_z)$ term
is resonant when its phase is stationary, $n_R\kappa + n_z\nu_z + m(\Omega-\Omega_p)=0$.
At corotation, $\Omega(\Lz)=\Omega_p$, this is the $n_R=n_z=0$ harmonic -- the part of
the spiral averaged over both the radial epicycle and the vertical orbit -- so the
resonant coupling is
%
\begin{equation}
  \Psi(\JR,\Jz) \;=\; \Phi_0(R_{\rm CR})\;\underbrace{J_0(k a_R)}_{G(\JR)}\;
  \underbrace{F(\Jz,k)}_{\text{vertical}},
  \,a_R=\sqrt{2\JR/\kappa},
  \label{eq:coupling}
\end{equation}
%
the product of the classical radial reduction factor $G(\JR)=J_0(ka_R)$
\citep[the Kalnajs/Bessel factor;][]{BinneyTremaine2008} and the vertical form
factor $F$. This coupling $\Psi$ is the amplitude of the corotation resonance -- the
depth of the pendulum well that traps migrating stars
(Equations~\ref{eq:pendulum}--\ref{eq:width} below) -- so its vertical factor $F$
directly sets how strongly a star of given $\Jz$ is captured and migrates.
\emph{The provenance bias is the $F$-factor of this coupling; the
\citet{DanielWyse2015} radial selectivity is the $G$-factor.} Equation
\eqref{eq:coupling} is the central identification of this paper: $F(\Jz,k)$ is the
exact vertical Fourier coefficient of the spiral at corotation.

Transforming to the rotating frame (the Jacobi integral $H_J=H_0-\Omega_p\Lz+
\Phi_s$ is conserved) and averaging over the fast angles $\theta_R,\theta_z$ -- for
which $\JR,\Jz$ are adiabatic invariants -- leaves the slow Hamiltonian. Expanding
about corotation in $p\equiv\Lz-L_{\rm CR}$, where $\mathrm{d}H_0/\mathrm{d}\Lz=
\Omega-\Omega_p$ vanishes, gives a pendulum
%
\begin{equation}
  \bar H(p,\theta_r) = \tfrac{1}{2}\,g\,p^2 + \Psi\cos\theta_r,
  \qquad g\equiv\left.\frac{\mathrm{d}\Omega}{\mathrm{d}\Lz}\right|_{\rm CR},
  \label{eq:pendulum}
\end{equation}
%
following the action-angle resonant-trapping construction of \citet{Monari2017},
here extended to carry the vertical degree of freedom through $\Psi$. The libration
(trapped, migrating) island has half-width in $p$
%
\begin{equation}
  \Delta p = 2\sqrt{\Psi/|g|} \;\propto\; \sqrt{\,F(\Jz,k)\,},
  \label{eq:width}
\end{equation}
%
so vertically cold stars (large $F$) sit in wider libration islands and are trapped
preferentially. Counting the trapped phase-space area gives a single-resonance
migration weight
%
\begin{equation}
  W(\Jz) \;\propto\; F(\Jz,k)^{1/2},
  \label{eq:wsqrtf}
\end{equation}
%
the island-area-counting law. The \emph{exact} trapped fraction follows from
integrating a Gaussian parent distribution of the slow action $p$, of dispersion
$\sigma_p$, over the separatrix:
%
\begin{equation}
  W(\Jz) = \frac{1}{\pi}\int_0^\pi \mathrm{d}u\,
  \mathrm{erf}\!\left(\frac{\kappa\sqrt{F(\Jz,k)}}{\sqrt2}\,\sin u\right),
  \label{eq:wexact}
\end{equation}
%
with a single dimensionless island-width parameter
$\kappa\equiv p_{\max}(F{=}1)/\sigma_p\propto\sqrt{|\Phi_s|}$.
Equation~\eqref{eq:wexact} reduces to the $\sqrt{F}$ law~\eqref{eq:wsqrtf} for
narrow islands ($\kappa\to0$) and saturates to unity for wide ones
(Figure~\ref{fig:trappedweight}). The provenance bias follows by reweighting the
equilibrium vertical distribution $\mathrm{d}N/\mathrm{d}\Ez\propto e^{-\Ez}/\nu_z$ by the
trapped weight $W(\Jz)$ and forming the migrator-to-parent dispersion ratio
$\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}$; Figure~\ref{fig:trappingcap} plots the resulting
log dispersion ratio $\ln\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}$ (negative; more negative is
stronger) at the Milky-Way thickness against the
island-width $\kappa$. It is \emph{most negative} in the dilute limit -- where
$W\propto\sqrt{F}$ gives the steepest vertical weighting -- and weakens (toward zero)
monotonically with $\kappa$: the exact trapped census therefore \emph{floors} the bias
at the $\sqrt{F}$ value, ${\approx}-7$ percent at the Milky-Way thickness, for any
spiral strength.
Finite spiral thickness softens this floor further: replacing $F$ by the
finite-thickness $F_{\rm soft}$~\eqref{eq:softened} weakens the ${\approx}-7$ percent
floor to ${\approx}-4$ to $-5.5$ percent across the plausible Milky-Way spiral thickness
$h\sim0.5$--$1$ (Figure~\ref{fig:thickness}), a $20$--$45$ percent reduction in magnitude.
Even the steeper \citet{DanielWyse2018} capture-fraction accounting, $W\propto F$,
reaches only ${\approx}-14$ percent. Neither single-resonance estimate produces the
strong cold-migrator preference seen in the simulations, in which the migrators are
drawn overwhelmingly from the kinematically cool population
\citep{VeraCiro2016imprint}; that preference points to the resonance-overlap regime
of Section~\ref{sec:overlap}, where $W$ steepens toward the diffusive $F^2$ limit. The effective weight $W=\exp[-s(1/F-1)]$
derived for the bias in Section~\ref{sec:bias} (Equation~\ref{eq:effweight}) is
accordingly a \emph{diffusive} effective model, not the single-resonance census.

\section{Resonance overlap and the diffusive regime}
\label{sec:overlap}

The single-resonance estimates of Section~\ref{sec:trapping} -- the exact trapped
census ($\sqrt{F}$, ${\approx}-7$ percent; Figure~\ref{fig:trappingcap}) and the
\citet{DanielWyse2018} capture fraction ($\propto F$, ${\approx}-14$ percent) --
are both too weak to produce the strong cold-migrator preference of the
simulations. Single isolated corotation trapping is therefore not the operative
regime. We test whether the Milky Way is
instead in the resonance-overlap (stochastic, diffusive) regime with the
\citet{Chirikov1979} criterion.

For a flat rotation curve ($\Omega=V_c/R$, $|g|=1/R^2$) the corotation libration
half-width~\eqref{eq:width} is a fixed fraction of the corotation radius,
%
\begin{equation}
  \Delta R / R = 2\sqrt{\epsilon\,G\,F},
  \qquad \epsilon \equiv |\Phi_s|/V_c^2 ,
\end{equation}
%
and two patterns with corotation radii $R_{\rm in}<R_{\rm out}$ overlap when the sum
of their island half-widths exceeds their separation,
%
\begin{equation}
  S \equiv \frac{\Delta R_{\rm in}+\Delta R_{\rm out}}{R_{\rm out}-R_{\rm in}}
  = 2\sqrt{\epsilon\,G\,F}\,\frac{\Omega_{\rm in}+\Omega_{\rm out}}
                                  {\Omega_{\rm in}-\Omega_{\rm out}} \ge 1 .
  \label{eq:chirikov}
\end{equation}
%
For the Milky Way bar ($\Omega_p\approx38\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,kpc^{-1}}$,
$R_{\rm CR}\approx6.0\kpc$) and spiral
($\Omega_p\approx20\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,kpc^{-1}}$, $R_{\rm CR}\approx11.5\kpc$),
Equation~\eqref{eq:chirikov} gives $S=0.64$, $0.91$, $1.12$ at $\epsilon=0.01$,
$0.02$, $0.03$ -- so at the physical spiral strength ($|\Phi_s|\sim1$--$3\%\,V_c^2$)
the bar and spiral corotation resonances sit at the overlap threshold $S\sim1$, the
mechanism of \citet{Minchev2010}.

Crucially, since $\Delta R\propto\sqrt{F}$, the overlap parameter scales as
$S\propto\sqrt{F}$: vertically cold stars cross the threshold first
(Figure~\ref{fig:overlap}). At the fiducial parameters the cold ($F=1$) population
overlaps at $\epsilon\approx0.024$, inside the physical band, while the hot
($F=0.5$) population stays in regular libration until $\epsilon\approx0.05$. The
diffusive churning channel is therefore itself biased toward vertically cold stars,
steepening the provenance bias beyond the single-resonance $F^{1/2}$ law toward the
$F^2$ diffusive limit. The Milky Way spiral sector lives at the crossover between
regular trapping and overlap diffusion, and that crossover is vertical-action
selective.

\paragraph{The crossover weight.} The two regimes are limits of one migration
weight. Quasilinear churning diffusion gives an angular-momentum diffusion
coefficient $D\propto|\Psi|^2\propto F^2$, so the diffusive weight is $W\propto F^2$
where resonances overlap, while the trapped census gives $W\propto F^{1/2}$ where
they do not. Modeling the weight as a power of the form factor whose exponent runs
from the trapping value $\tfrac12$ to the diffusive value $2$ through a gate centered
on the threshold $S=1$,
%
\begin{equation}
  W(\Jz)=F^{\,p},\quad p=\tfrac12+\tfrac32\,\frac{S^2}{1+S^2},\quad
  S(\Jz)=S_0\sqrt{F(\Jz,k)},
  \label{eq:crossover}
\end{equation}
%
with the single physical parameter $S_0$ the effective overlap, the predicted bias
deepens monotonically from the $\sqrt{F}$ value (${\approx}-7$ percent) to the $F^2$
value (${\approx}-27$ percent) as $S_0$ grows (Figure~\ref{fig:crossover}). The bar
and spiral alone ($S_0\approx1$) give ${\approx}-11$ percent; a ${\sim}-22$ percent
bias -- the level of the \citet{VeraCiro2016imprint} cool-migrator selection -- is
reached at $S_0\approx2$--$3$, the extra overlap expected once the Milky Way's
recurrent transient spirals are included. The crossover
sits at the critical form factor $F_{\rm crit}=1/S_0^2$ -- a critical vertical
action separating diffusive (cold) from confined (hot) migrators, a structural
prediction. The endpoints are physical; the gate is the minimal model of the
crossover sharpness.

\paragraph{First-principles diffusive weight.} The diffusive endpoint is not assumed
but follows from kinetic theory. The inhomogeneous Balescu--Lenard corotation
diffusion coefficient in angular momentum,
$D_{\Lz}(J)=\pi\sum_m|\Psi_m(J)|^2 C_m(\Omega)$
\citep{Heyvaerts2010,Fouvry2015}, inherits the
$|\Psi_m|^2\propto F^2$ of the resonant coupling~\eqref{eq:coupling}, so the diffusive
migration weight is $W\propto F^2$. A finite libration width
$\Delta\omega\propto\sqrt{\Psi}\propto\sqrt{F}$ broadens the resonant denominator
\citep{Dupree1966}; for a Lorentzian spiral spectrum of width $W_s$ this yields the
closed form
%
\begin{equation}
  W(\Jz)=\frac{F^2}{1+b\sqrt{F}},\qquad b=\frac{\Delta\omega}{W_s},
  \label{eq:bl}
\end{equation}
%
interpolating the decorrelated quasilinear limit ($W\propto F^2$, $b\to0$) and the
coherent, resonance-broadened limit ($W\propto F^{3/2}$, $b\to\infty$). The provenance
bias in this diffusive regime is therefore confined to a narrow \emph{derived} band,
${\approx}-20$ to $-27$ percent at the Galactic thickness, almost independent of $b$
(Figure~\ref{fig:bl}): the observed bias follows from the disk being in the diffusive
(overlap) regime, with no tuned parameter. The crossover weight~\eqref{eq:crossover}
thus bridges the derived $\sqrt{F}$ value (${\approx}-7$ percent) and this derived
diffusive band.

\paragraph{Test-particle validation.} The mechanism is confirmed by direct orbit
integration, with no analytic input. Integrating ${\sim}3000$ test particles in
\texttt{MWPotential2014} \citep{Bovy2015} plus an imposed transient spiral that falls
off vertically over a scale height $H$, and weighting each star by its churning
$|\Delta\Lz|$, recovers the provenance bias (Figure~\ref{fig:testparticle}). Here $H$
is the spiral's vertical scale -- the simulation's stand-in for the analytic reach
$1/k$ of the $e^{-k|z|}$ fall-off, distinct from the disc scale height $z_0=\hZ$ -- so
the simulation's dimensionless thinness $\alpha\equiv z_0/H$ exactly mirrors the
analytic $\alpha=kz_0$ (the ``${\sim}$'' reflecting only that the galpy vertical
profile is not a pure $e^{-k|z|}$). Migrators are vertically colder, the bias
strengthens with $\alpha\sim z_0/H$ in agreement with the single-resonance prediction
-- the log
dispersion ratio reaching $\ln\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}\approx-0.06$ (migrators
${\approx}6$ percent colder) near the Galactic $\alpha\sim1$ -- and stacking $M$
overlapping patterns of different pattern speed deepens the bias monotonically,
directly confirming the resonance-overlap mechanism of Section~\ref{sec:overlap}.

\section{The provenance bias and the $\JR^{\max}$--$\hZ$ relation}
\label{sec:bias}

\paragraph{Bias magnitude.} In the diffusive regime a star is captured into
migration with a probability set by the competition between its radial random energy
$\sigR^2$ and the resonant coupling energy $|\Phi_s|\,F$ -- the spiral amplitude
reduced by the vertical form factor (Equation~\ref{eq:coupling}). A Boltzmann capture
probability $\propto\exp(-\sigR^2/|\Phi_s|F)$, normalized to a mid-plane orbit
($F=1$), gives the effective migration weight
%
\begin{equation}
  W(\Jz)=\exp\!\left[-s\left(\frac{1}{F(\Jz,k)}-1\right)\right],
  \qquad s\equiv\frac{\sigR^2}{|\Phi_s|},
  \label{eq:effweight}
\end{equation}
%
the smooth, three-dimensional form of the \citet{DanielWyse2015} random-energy
capture criterion. Applying the reweighting of Section~\ref{sec:trapping} with this
$W$ yields the migrator dispersion ratio $\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}$. At the Milky-Way thickness $\alpha=0.84$ a
bias of ${\approx}-22$ percent ($\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}\approx0.80$, i.e.
$\ln\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}\approx-0.22$, the cool-star
threshold of \citealt{VeraCiro2016imprint}) corresponds to $s=0.90$ -- inside the
physical range $s\sim0.8$--$2$ ($|\Phi_s|\sim1$--$3\%\,V_c^2$); the model predicts
the bias weakens as the spiral strengthens and strengthens with disk thickness
$\alpha$ (Figure~\ref{fig:bias}), matching the strength dependence of
\citet{Mikkola2020} and the morphology independence of \citet{VeraCiro2016imprint}. As
Section~\ref{sec:overlap} makes explicit, the steepness encoded in $s$ is the
signature of the diffusive (overlap) regime rather than of clean single-resonance
trapping: this effective weight is the phenomenological counterpart of the crossover
weight~\eqref{eq:crossover} at $S_0\approx2$--$3$.

  $\ln\sigz^{\rm mig}/\sigz^{\rm all}$ versus disk thickness $\alpha=k\hZ$, shown as a
  band over the physical spiral-strength range $s=0.8$--$2$
  ($|\Phi_s|\sim1$--$3\%\,V_c^2$). The bias deepens with thickness; at the Milky-Way

\paragraph{Structural slope.} Combining the isothermal-sheet scale height
$\hZ=\sigz^2/(\pi G\Sigma)$ with the epicyclic action
$\JR^{\max}=c_{\max}\sigR^2/\kappa$ gives the slope as the squared
radial-to-vertical heating-anisotropy ratio,
%
\begin{equation}
  a \;=\; \frac{\JR^{\max}}{\hZ}
    \;=\; c_{\max}\,\frac{\pi G\Sigma}{\kappa}\left(\frac{\sigR}{\sigz}\right)^2 .
  \label{eq:slope}
\end{equation}
%
For solar-neighborhood values ($\Sigma=50\,M_\odot\,\mathrm{pc}^{-2}$,
$\kappa=0.037\,\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}\,pc^{-1}}$, $\sigR=35$, $\sigz=18\,
\mathrm{km\,s^{-1}}$) and $c_{\max}=1$, Equation~\eqref{eq:slope} gives
$3.6\times10^{-2}\,\Lsun/\kpc$, within a few percent of the measured
$3.7\times10^{-2}$. Equation~\eqref{eq:slope} uses the self-gravitating
$\mathrm{sech}^2$ sheet, whereas \citet{Palicio2024} fit $\hZ$ as an exponential
vertical scale-length; the two scales differ by an order-unity factor, which we
absorb into $c_{\max}$.

\paragraph{Age-running.} Because $\Sigma$ and $\kappa$ are structural, the slope's
only population dependence is $(\sigR/\sigz)^2$. Each channel obeys an
age--velocity-dispersion relation $\sigma\propto\mathrm{age}^\beta$, so
%
\begin{equation}
  a(\mathrm{age}) \;\propto\; \mathrm{age}^{\,2(\beta_R-\beta_z)} .
  \label{eq:agerun}
\end{equation}
%
With $\beta_z\approx0.5$ and $\beta_R\approx0.35$
\citep{AumerBinneySchonrich2016,Sun2024}, the exponent is
$-0.3$, so the slope of an $8\,$Gyr population is $\approx0.54$ times that of a
$1\,$Gyr population: \emph{the relation should flatten with age}. Re-fitting the
$\JR^{\max}$--$\hZ$ relation in mono-age or mono-abundance bins thus measures
$\beta_R-\beta_z$ from spiral-arm kinematics; an age-independent slope would instead
require $\beta_R=\beta_z$.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The provenance bias, the spiral $\JR^{\max}$--$\hZ$ relation, and the vertical
selectivity of corotation capture are tied together by a single analytic object, the
vertical form factor $F(k\hZ)$, which Equation~\eqref{eq:coupling} identifies as the
exact vertical Fourier coefficient of the spiral in the corotation-resonant
Hamiltonian. $F$ is the common thread, not the sole determinant of each magnitude:
the bias size additionally requires the migration weight set by the resonance regime
(Sections~\ref{sec:trapping}--\ref{sec:overlap}), while the
$\JR^{\max}$--$\hZ$ slope follows from the structural anisotropy factor
$\pi G\Sigma/\kappa\,(\sigR/\sigz)^2$ -- the form factor does not enter the slope
directly, its role being to select the cold-migrator population that defines
$\JR^{\max}$ and to share the same disk thickness $\alpha=k\hZ$.
Its radial twin is the classical \citet{DanielWyse2015} in-plane
selectivity; the $e^{-k|z|}$ reduction is standard \citep{BinneyTremaine2008}; and
the bias was found in simulations \citep{VeraCiro2016imprint,Mikkola2020}. What was
missing is the vertical extension that turns the verbal mechanism into
Equations~\eqref{eq:coupling}--\eqref{eq:wsqrtf} and connects it to
\citet{Palicio2024}. The form factor should not be conflated with the
finite-thickness reduction factors of classical density-wave theory
\citep{Toomre1964}, which weaken a self-gravitating disk's \emph{collective}
response to a density wave: $F$ is instead the orbit-average of an externally
imposed spiral felt by a \emph{single} star at fixed $\Jz$ -- a test-particle
coupling, not a modification of the self-gravitating response.

A complementary first-principles link between migration and vertical structure
already exists: \citet{Roskar2013} show, from vertical-action conservation, that an
outward migrator's scale height grows as $h_{Z,f}/h_{Z,i}=\exp(\Delta R/2R_d)$.
That law governs the \emph{fate} of a star once it has migrated; the form factor
governs the \emph{selectivity} of capture -- which stars migrate at all. The two are
distinct and complementary, and only the latter produces the provenance bias.

The two dynamical regimes the form factor distinguishes -- single-resonance
trapping ($W\propto F^{1/2}$) and resonance-overlap diffusion ($W$ steeper) -- are
the two ends of one axis. The diffusive end is the inhomogeneous Balescu--Lenard
description of secular evolution in tightly wound disks \citep{Fouvry2015}, and the
linear-response perturbed distribution function of \citet{MonariFamaeySiebert2016}
is its complement; a full treatment would compute $W(\Jz)$ as the phase-space
trapped fraction across the overlap transition rather than in its two limits, which
we defer.

Several caveats bound the calculation. The finite-thickness softening of the
$e^{-k|z|}$ kernel (Section~\ref{sec:formfactor}, Equation~\eqref{eq:softened}) is an
order-unity correction at the Galactic $\alpha\sim1$, carried here for an exponential
spiral profile; a self-consistent profile would refine it. The overlap parameter
\eqref{eq:chirikov} is sensitive to the bar--spiral pattern-speed separation; the
well-separated fiducial adopted here is conservative, and the Milky Way's recurrent
transient spirals add resonances that only increase the overlap. We also stress that
the ${\sim}-22$ percent figure is the cool-star color threshold of
\citet{VeraCiro2016imprint}'s Fig.~4, not a measured migrator dispersion offset; the
provenance-bias magnitude is not yet robustly measured. Our conclusions rest on the
regime ordering -- single-resonance trapping floors near $-7$ percent, the diffusive
regime predicts $-20$ to $-27$ percent -- and on the qualitative cold-migrator
preference, rather than on a precise value. The age-running of the
slope \eqref{eq:agerun} is, to our knowledge, an untested and clean prediction for
current Gaia--APOGEE--LAMOST data.
