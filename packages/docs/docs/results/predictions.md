# Predictions

**The slope runs with age.** Since $\Sigma$ and $\kappa$ are structural, the
slope's only population dependence is $(\sigma_R/\sigma_z)^2$. Each channel obeys
$\sigma\propto\mathrm{age}^\beta$, so

$$
a(\mathrm{age}) \propto \mathrm{age}^{\,2(\beta_R-\beta_z)} .
$$

With $\beta_z\approx0.5$ (Sun et al. 2024) and $\beta_R\approx0.35$, the exponent
is $-0.3$: the slope of an 8 Gyr population is $\approx0.54$ times that of a 1 Gyr
population. **The $J_R^{\max}$--$h_Z$ relation should flatten with age** -- a
falsifiable test in mono-age / mono-abundance Gaia–APOGEE–LAMOST bins. An
age-independent slope would instead require $\beta_R=\beta_z$.

**Open items.** The $e^{-k|z|}$ fall-off is the razor-thin leading term; at the
Galactic $\alpha\sim1$ a finite-thickness response softens it at the order-unity
level. The high-$J_R$ tail defining $J_R^{\max}$ is sampled from the cold migrator
population, so the slope carries a selection factor of order the bias itself.
