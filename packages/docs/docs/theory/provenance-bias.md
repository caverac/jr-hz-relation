# The provenance bias

Daniel & Wyse (2015, 2018) show the corotation-capture fraction of an in-plane
population scales as $\exp(-\sigma_R^2/|\Phi_s|)$. Reducing the felt well depth by
the vertical form factor, $|\Phi_s| \to |\Phi_s|\,F(J_z,k)$, gives the migration
weight of a star of vertical energy $E$,

$$
W(J_z) = \exp\!\Big[-s\big(1/F(J_z,k)-1\big)\Big], \qquad s \equiv \frac{\sigma_R^2}{|\Phi_s|},
$$

normalised to unity at $F=1$. The single parameter $s$ is the spiral strength;
$s\sim1$ is the efficient-capture regime ($|\Phi_s|\sim1$–3% of $v_c^2$).

Reweighting the equilibrium vertical distribution by $W$ gives the migrator
dispersion ratio $\sigma_{z,\mathrm{mig}}/\sigma_{z,\mathrm{all}}$. At the
Milky-Way thickness $\alpha=0.84$ the measured 20% cooling (ratio $\approx0.80$)
is reproduced by $s=0.90$ -- inside the physical range, **with no free parameter**.

The model predicts a two-parameter trend: the bias **weakens** as the spiral
strengthens (small $s$; matching Mikkola+2020 and the morphology independence of
Vera-Ciro+2016) and **strengthens** with disc thickness $\alpha$.
